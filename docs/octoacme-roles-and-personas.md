# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers design and validate user experience flows, prototypes, and UI elements. They collaborate closely with Product Managers and Developers to ensure user-centric design decisions, advocating for usability, accessibility, and continuous user feedback.

### Responsibilities
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Define and maintain design systems and UI guidelines
- Review implemented features for design fidelity and accessibility
- Advocate for the end user throughout the product lifecycle

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and consistent user experiences
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction
- Ensure designs are feasible for implementation within project constraints

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers and Developers
- Usability test reports and findings shared with the team
- Collaboration in sprint planning to align design milestones with development

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Works with **Product Managers** to understand user stories and acceptance criteria
- Provides iterative feedback to **Developers** during implementation
- Coordinates with **QA Lead** on usability test scenarios and acceptance

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
The QA Lead owns the overall quality strategy for the project. They plan test coverage, oversee testing activities, and drive defect triage. The QA Lead works with Developers, Product, and Project Managers to uphold the Definition of Done and ensure release readiness.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the test strategy and quality standards
- Plan and coordinate test phases (unit, integration, regression, UAT)
- Track and triage defects, escalating blockers as needed
- Ensure CI pipelines include adequate automated test coverage
- Review acceptance criteria and validate that features meet the Definition of Done

### Goals
- Prevent regressions and maintain high product quality
- Shift testing left to catch issues early in the development cycle
- Provide clear release readiness signals to Project and Product Managers

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and quality status reports shared with the project team
- Defect triage meetings with Developers and Project Manager
- Pre-release sign-off documentation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Aligns with **Project Manager** on major milestones and test phases
- Partners with **Developers** for automated test coverage and bug fixes
- Reviews requirements and acceptance criteria with **Product Manager**
- Coordinates usability test scenarios with **UX Designer**

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
The Release Manager coordinates all steps needed to ship releases safely and on schedule. They own communication plans, deployment scheduling, release notes, and rollback/incident processes to ensure smooth and predictable releases.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule releases in collaboration with Project and Product Managers
- Maintain and execute the release and deployment checklist
- Draft and distribute release notes and announcements
- Coordinate rollback plans and support incident response if a release fails
- Facilitate post-release reviews and capture action items

### Goals
- Deliver releases on schedule with minimal disruption
- Ensure all stakeholders are informed and prepared for each release
- Reduce release risk through thorough planning and validation

### Typical Communication
- Release schedule and deployment plan shared ahead of each release
- Release notes distributed to stakeholders and support teams
- Post-release summary and retrospective notes

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Works with **Project Manager** and **Product Manager** to schedule and document releases
- Ensures release checklist items are completed by **Developers** and **QA Lead**
- Informs **Stakeholders** and supports post-release reviews

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts translate business needs into detailed requirements and documentation. They bridge communications between technical teams and non-technical stakeholders, helping ensure alignment on goals, deliverables, and success criteria.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements from stakeholders and end users
- Translate requirements into user stories, acceptance criteria, and process flows
- Facilitate requirements workshops and review sessions
- Identify gaps or ambiguities in requirements and drive resolution
- Maintain traceability between business goals and delivered features

### Goals
- Ensure all requirements are clear, complete, and testable before development begins
- Reduce rework caused by misunderstood or incomplete requirements
- Strengthen alignment between business stakeholders and the delivery team

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documents, user stories, and process diagrams
- Stakeholder review sessions and sign-off meetings
- Regular updates to Product Manager and Project Manager on requirements status

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Gathers needs from **Stakeholders** and end users
- Clarifies requirements for **Developers** and **QA Lead**
- Reports findings and recommendations back to **Product Manager**
